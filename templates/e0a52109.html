What are Manciples?

Manciples were historically known as a type of slave who was responsible for the care and management of livestock. However, the term has evolved over time to refer to a variety of roles in agriculture and animal husbandry. This forum is dedicated to learning and discussing the history and use of manciples.

History of Manciples

The word "manciple" comes from the Latin word "mancipium," which means "slave." Manciples were originally slaves who were responsible for the care and management of livestock. They were typically purchased by the owner of the livestock and were considered a valuable asset. Over time, the role of the manciple evolved and they became more involved in the management of agricultural estates.

Usage of Manciples

Today, manciples can refer to a variety of roles in agriculture and animal husbandry. They may be responsible for the care and management of livestock, the running of a farm or agricultural estate, or the management of food production and distribution. Manciples are still an important part of many agricultural and food-based industries.
