Phrenospasm, Idola, and Causelessness

Phrenospasm refers to a sudden contraction or spasm of the muscles of the diaphragm, causing a sharp intake of breath. Idola are the false images or notions that are generated by the mind, which can lead to errors in perception and judgment. Causelessness refers to the lack of a discernible cause or reason for something.

This website is dedicated to exploring the meanings behind these three intriguing words. We will delve into their origins, their usage throughout history, and their modern-day interpretations.
