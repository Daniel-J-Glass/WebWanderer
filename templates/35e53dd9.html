Ancient Porridge Recipes

Porridge is a dish made by boiling grains or legumes in milk or water. It has been a staple food throughout history and has been consumed in various forms across cultures. This website explores the history of ancient porridge recipes and their cultural significance. From the simple barley porridge of ancient Rome to the spicy oatmeal of medieval Scotland, we delve into the origins and evolution of this timeless dish.

Ancient Roman Barley Porridge

The ancient Romans were one of the first civilizations to consume porridge on a regular basis. Theirs was a simple recipe made from barley, water, and salt. This porridge was a staple food for Roman soldiers and was also consumed by the common people. It was often eaten with vegetables and meat.

Medieval Scottish Oatmeal Porridge

Oatmeal porridge has been a popular dish in Scotland for centuries. In medieval times, it was often served with spices such as cinnamon and ginger. Scottish oatmeal porridge was also a staple food for soldiers and was often eaten with meat and vegetables. Today, it is still a popular dish in Scotland and is often served with honey or jam.
